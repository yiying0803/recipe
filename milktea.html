Ingredients:

  • 125g dried pearls (I use the black pearls)
  • Water, for par boiling the pearls in
  • Ice water, for cooling the pearls
  • 120g black sugar (see notes above)
  • 420g water (for syrup)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 6 tea bags
  • 1 litre water (for tea)
  • 9tbsp non-dairy creamer

Instructions:

  1. Bring a medium size pot of water to the boil, once the water is at a rolling boil, add the pearls and boil for 3 minutes on high stirring occasionally to ensure they do not stick
  2. Immediately transfer to a bowl of ice water to shock the pearls and to stop the cooking process. This also helps with ensuring a chewier consistency.
  3. In a medium pot, add the black sugar and salt along with the water for the syrup. Bring to a simmer, drain the black pearls from the ice bath and add them to the sugar syrup. Skim the foam that rises to the top of the syrup to ensure a cleaner and less cloudy/starchy syrup. Simmer for 7 mins or until soft and provides little resistance. Stir occasionally to ensure that the pearls do not stick to one another.
  4. Strain and transfer to a bowl, add some of the sugar syrup until it just cover the pearls and set aside.
  5. Meanwhile, reduce the remaining sugar syrup to a glaze that coats the pot when you tilt it leaving streaks behind. Transfer to jar/bowl and set aside to cool. It will thicken up as it cools.
  6. Bring 1 litre of water to a boil and add the tea bags. Simmer for 10 minutes covered with a lid, turn off the flame and let sit in the hot water with the lid on for another 10 minutes. Give it a stir occasionally to help with ensuring that the tea is slowly getting more concentrated as it sits. Remove the tea bags using a spoon to press out as much of the “flavour” as possible.
  7. Transfer tea to a blender, add the non-dairy creamer and blend on low until incorporated.
  8. Add 1.5 tbsp of syrup to your mug, to get the streaks, tilt your cup and rotate it to allow the syrup to coat the glass. Add 2 tbsp of pearls to the glass, add ice to you liking and fill it up with the milk tea. Add more syrup to taste.
